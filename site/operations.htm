The Iranian Business School is a wholly independent, not-for-profit organisation to be established in the Caspian Sea region of northern Iran.  The project is currently administered by a Steering Committee based in Iran and the United Kingdom. The Committee is responsible for strategic planning, fundraising, curriculum development and the whole process of setting up the School.

Notwithstanding its private status, the School shall ultimately operate under the auspices of the Iranian Ministry of Science, Research and Technology whose involvement will ensure that the School conforms to the legal requirements of independent educational establishments in Iran.

Mostafa Karimian-Eghbal
 

MSc. and PhD from University of California Davis (1992).  Served as the President of the Isfahan Science and Technology Town (2001-2004) and Deputy Minister of Technology at the Ministry of Science, Research and Technology of Iran (2004-2006).  Presently Associate Professor at Tarbiat Modares University in Iran. 
   

Roshanak Khalili-Dwyer
 

MA in International Relations from Columbia University and MSc. from the London Business School’s Sloan Fellowship Programme.  With seven years experience at Bankers Trust Company in New York and London, is currently an advisor to the Board of Directors of Butane Industrial Group in Tehran and serves as a director at the Institute for Family Business UK; the London Business School Regional Advisory Board and the Board of Directors of Iran Heritage Foundation.
   

Mohit Mamudi
 

BA from Abadan Institute of Technology and MBA from Boston University.  Has worked as a management and marketing executive or consultant with more than 20 Fortune 500 corporations in Europe, China, Latin America, and MENA region.  Currently Chairman of Amacom UK Ltd and Director of several other companies.  With a number of publications in strategic marketing and CSR, has lectured in postgraduate and management training programmes in UK, USA, Iran, Egypt and Russia. He is a founder and director of the Centre for Development Management Studies (CEDEMAS).
   

Reza Moradi-Aliarabi (Project Director)
 

BSc. and MSc. in Engineering from Isfahan University of Technology.  The co-founder and Chairman of the Iron and Steel Society of Iran, has been VP for Executive Affairs at the Isfahan Science & Technology Town and a board member of the Technology Network of Isfahan.  Has taught several university level courses and is widely published in the field of Science Parks Management.
   

Rouzbeh Pirouz (Chair)
 

BA from Stanford University (1993) and the Kennedy School of Government of Harvard University (1996), Rhodes Scholar at Oxford University (1996-1999). Co-founder and Chief Executive Officer of a leading European technology firm, Mondus Ltd which was acquired by Telecom Italia in 2002.  Currently Senior Partner at Pelican Partners, a private equity and property investment firm with operations in Europe and the Middle East.  He is a member of the Board of Directors of  Iran Heritage Foundation
   

Seena Rejal
 

MSc and Ph.D. from the University of Cambridge in Manufacturing Engineering, Economics and Policy. Is Director of Cambridge Policy Associates Ltd., a consultancy focusing on government-level industrial and technological policy formulation, and a technology entrepreneur in the renewable energies sector. He is also involved in national and university-level entrepreneurship development initiatives worldwide.
