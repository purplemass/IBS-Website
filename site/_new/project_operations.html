How We Operate

The Iranian Business School will operate as a wholly independent, not-for-profit organisation and is expected to be established in Tehran. The Project is currently administered by a Steering Committee based in Iran and the United Kingdom. The Committee is responsible for strategic planning, fundraising, curriculum development and all other activities involved in the process of setting up the School.

Notwithstanding its private status, the School shall ultimately operate under the auspices of the Iranian Ministry of Science, Research and Technology whose involvement will ensure that the School conforms to the legal requirements of independent educational establishments in Iran.

It is expected that the School will become operational in September 2010, subject to receiving the necessary licenses and approvals from the relevant authorities.

Roshanak Khalili-Dwyer MA in International Relations from Columbia University and MSc. from the London Business School's Sloan Fellowship Programme. With seven years experience at Bankers Trust Company in New York and London, is currently an advisor to the Board of Directors of Butane Industrial Group in Tehran and serves as a director at the Institute for Family Business UK; the London Business School Regional Advisory Board and the Board of Directors of Iran Heritage Foundation.

Mohit Mamudi BA from Abadan Institute of Technology and MBA from Boston University. Has worked as a management and marketing executive or consultant with more than 20 Fortune 500 corporations in Europe, China, Latin America, and MENA region. Currently Chairman of Amacom UK Ltd and Director of several other companies. With a number of publications in strategic marketing and CSR, has lectured in postgraduate and management training programmes in UK, USA, Iran, Egypt and Russia. He is a founder and director of the Centre for Development Management Studies (CEDEMAS).

Reza Moradi-Aliarabi (Project Director) BSc. and MSc. in Engineering from Isfahan University of Technology. The co-founder and Chairman of the Iron and Steel Society of Iran, has been VP for Executive Affairs at the Isfahan Science & Technology Town and a board member of the Technology Network of Isfahan. Has taught several university level courses and is widely published in the field of Science Parks Management.

Rouzbeh Pirouz (Chair) Born in Iran, Mr. Pirouz is currently senior partner at Pelican Partners, a private equity and property investment firm in London and Chairman of Turquoise Partners, a leading investment company in Iran. Prior to this, he co-founded and managed Mondus, a leading European company in the area of business-to-business electronic procurement. Mr. Pirouz has a Bachelor's degree from Stanford University and a Master's degree from Harvard University. He was also a Rhodes Scholar at Oxford University. He sits on the board of a number of leading charities in the UK and is a Young Global Leader of the World Economic Forum (Davos, Switzerland).

Seena Rejal MSc and Ph.D. from the University of Cambridge in Manufacturing Engineering, Economics and Policy. Is Director of Cambridge Policy Associates Ltd., a consultancy focusing on government-level industrial and technological policy formulation, and a technology entrepreneur in the renewable energies sector. He is also involved in national and university-level entrepreneurship development initiatives worldwide.
