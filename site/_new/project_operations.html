How We Operate

The Iranian Business School will operate as a wholly independent, not-for-profit organisation and is expected to be established in Tehran. The Project is currently administered by a Steering Committee based in Iran and the United Kingdom. The Committee is responsible for strategic planning, fundraising, curriculum development and all other activities involved in the process of setting up the School.

Notwithstanding its private status, the School shall ultimately operate under the auspices of the Iranian Ministry of Science, Research and Technology whose involvement will ensure that the School conforms to the legal requirements of independent educational establishments in Iran.

It is expected that the School will become operational in September 2010, subject to receiving the necessary licenses and approvals from the relevant authorities.

Steering Committee Members

Professor Michael Hay Special Advisor & Member of: Executive Committee - London Michael Hay is Professor of Management Practice in Entrepreneurship at London Business School. He was Deputy Dean of the school for five years until December 2006, and Acting Dean of CIDA City Campus in Johannesburg in 2007. He joined the London Business School faculty in 1987 and was Director of the Foundation for Entrepreneurial Management, Associate Dean of the Sloan Masters Programme and Dean of Executive Education before taking the role of Deputy Dean. He is co-founder of the Global Entrepreneurship Monitor, which examines the nature and impact of entrepreneurial activity in more than 40 countries around the world, and Chairman of the Board of the Centre for Scientific Enterprise, a major collaborative programme between London Business School and University College London. He has undertaken assignments for many international companies including British Airways, 3M and United Business Media both in Europe and the USA. He is a founding director of Imparta Limited, director of Capricorn Venture Partners in Leuven and author of numerous articles and books. He co-founded and was Chief Executive Officer of a leading European technology firm, Mondus Ltd.
